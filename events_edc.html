Circuitronix!

About

It's a three round contest

  • Round-1:Design Challenge (Usage of internet is permitted).
  • Round-2:Build your logic for the problem statement and simulate using a Design platform.
  • Round-3: Infer the given circuit and tell us what it does!

Note

All the required components will be provided during the event.

Judging Criteria

  • Time taken
  • Novelty in logic building.
  • Method of implementation.
  • Efficiency of the solution.
  • Simulation and hardware results.

Prerequisites

Basic circuitry knowledge, Interest in circuit designing.

Team Size

3 members(max)

Duration

2.5 Hrs

Prizes worth 4K*

*Terms and conditions apply

Eureka!

Description

  • A real time problem statement will be given during the event. Innovative solutions must be discovered for them accordingly.
  • An hour will be given to analyse and compare with the existing technologies, find alternatives and provide arguments in support of your idea. Usage of internet is allowed.
  • The solution must be presented on PowerPoint and sold to the judges.
  • Evaluation will be based on originality, feasibility of implementation, marketing strategy, innovation, novelty and efficiency of the solution.

Judging Criteria

  • The understanding of problem statement.
  • Novelty in solution.
  • Efficiency and feasibility of the solution.
  • Pitching strategy of your solution to the judges.

Requirements

Laptop installed with Ansys. Designs to be simulated will be provided on-site. (For those without laptop, a lab with Ansys installed will be arranged.)

Team Size

3 members(max)

No prerequisites, just the enthusiasm to squeeze your brain for crazy new ideas.

Buzz Wire Loop

Description

Buzz wire loop game is a game which involves guiding a metal loop (a 'probe') along a serpentine length of wire without touching the loop to the wire. The loop and wire are connected to a power source in such a way that, if they touch, they form a closed electric circuit. The circuit is connected to a light or sound-emitting device of some sort, so that when the loop and the wire touch, the light-emitting device will light up, and the sound-emitting device will make a sound, traditionally a buzzing noise.
